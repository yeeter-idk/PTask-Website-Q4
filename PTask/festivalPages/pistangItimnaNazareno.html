Pista ng Itim na Nazareno

— Quiapo, NCR (Metro Manila)

When: January 9

In Honor Of: The Black Nazarene, a dark wooden statue of Jesus Christ carrying the cross

Highlights:
  • The Traslación, a grand procession of the statue
  • Millions of devotees joining the procession barefoot
  • Prayer gatherings and religious masses
  • Acts of devotion and thanksgiving
Black Nazarene Feast (Pista ng Itim na Nazareno)
The Feast of the Black Nazarene is a major religious event celebrated every January 9 in Quiapo, Manila. It honors the Black Nazarene, a dark wooden statue of Jesus Christ carrying the cross, enshrined in the Quiapo Church (Minor Basilica of the Black Nazarene). Significance: The feast reflects the deep faith and devotion of millions of Filipino devotees who gather in Quiapo to participate in prayers, processions, and acts of thanksgiving.

Purposes:
  • To express deep faith and devotion
  • To seek blessings, healing, and guidance
  • To strengthen religious unity among Filipinos
