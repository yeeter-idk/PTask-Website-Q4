Bonok-Bonok Maradjaw Karadjaw Festival

— Surigao City, Region XIII - Caraga

When: September (highlight on September 10)

In Honor Of: Sr. San Nicolas de Tolentino

Highlights:
  • Street dancing competition
  • Ritual dances inspired by the Mamanwa tribe
  • Cultural shows and parades
  • Religious procession
Bonok-Bonok Maradjaw Karadjaw Festival
The Bonok-Bonok Maradjaw Karadjaw Festival is a colorful cultural and religious celebration held in Surigao City, located in the Caraga region. “Maradjaw Karadjaw” means “very good” in the local dialect, reflecting the joyful spirit of the festival. Significance: The festival highlights Surigao’s rich indigenous heritage and strong religious devotion through cultural performances, rituals, and community celebrations.

Purposes:
  • To honor Sr. San Nicolas de Tolentino with religious devotion
  • To showcase Surigao’s rich indigenous Mamanwa heritage
  • To celebrate joy, community spirit, and cultural identity (“Maradjaw Karadjaw” means “very good”)
