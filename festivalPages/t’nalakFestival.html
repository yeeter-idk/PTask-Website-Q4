T’nalak Festival

— Koronadal, Region XII - SOCCSKSARGEN

When: July (highlight on July 18)

In Honor Of: Foundation Anniversary of South Cotabato

Highlights:
  • Street dancing and cultural competitions
  • Showcasing of T’nalak cloth woven by the T'boli tribe
  • Trade fairs and concerts
  • Cultural presentations from different ethnic groups
T’nalak Festival
The T’nalak Festival is a vibrant cultural celebration held every July in Koronadal, located in the SOCCSKSARGEN region. The festival is named after T’nalak, a sacred woven fabric made from abaca fibers by the T'boli people. Significance: The T’nalak cloth symbolizes unity, heritage, and cultural pride among the people of South Cotabato and reflects the rich traditions of the T'boli tribe.

Purposes:
  • To honor the foundation anniversary of South Cotabato
  • To celebrate the sacred T’boli weaving tradition and cultural heritage
  • To promote unity, heritage, and cultural pride through the T’nalak cloth
