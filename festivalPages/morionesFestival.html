Moriones Festival

— Marinduque, MIMAROPA

When: Holy Week (Palm Sunday to Easter Sunday)

In Honor Of: Saint Longinus, the Roman centurion who converted to Christianity

Highlights:
  • Moriones procession and street performances by masked Roman soldiers
  • Via Crucis (Way of the Cross) reenactment on Good Friday
  • Pugutan (beheading of Longinus) on Black Saturday or Easter Sunday
  • Senakulo (Passion play) performances
  • Traditional putong welcome ceremony
  • Colorful hand-painted wooden masks and Roman-inspired costumes
Moriones Festival
The Moriones Festival is a vibrant Lenten and religious celebration held annually during Holy Week on the island of Marinduque, located in the MIMAROPA region. The festival is named after the “morion,” a helmet worn by participants who portray Roman soldiers in dramatic street performances and religious reenactments. Significance: During the festival, the towns of Boac, Mogpog, Gasan, Buenavista, Santa Cruz, and Torrijos transform into stages for a unique folk-religious tradition that combines faith, history, and artistic mask-making.

Purposes:
  • To reenact the story of Saint Longinus, the Roman centurion associated with the crucifixion of Christ
  • To serve as a form of panata (vow or penance) for devotees
  • To celebrate Marinduque’s religious heritage and craftsmanship in mask-making
  • To promote cultural identity and community unity through shared Lenten traditions
