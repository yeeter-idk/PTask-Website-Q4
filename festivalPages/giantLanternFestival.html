Giant Lantern Festival

— San Fernando, Pampanga, Region III - Central Luzon

When: December (Christmas season)

In Honor Of: San Fernando’s title as the “Christmas Capital of the Philippines”

Highlights:
  • Huge, colorful lanterns called parol
  • Lanterns measuring up to 20 feet in diameter
  • Each barangay creates its own giant lantern
  • Lanterns decorated with thousands of lights that move in synchronized patterns
Giant Lantern Festival (Ligligan Parul)
The Giant Lantern Festival, locally known as Ligligan Parul, is a famous Christmas festival celebrated every December in San Fernando, Pampanga. San Fernando is called the “Christmas Capital of the Philippines” because of this grand event. Significance: The festival showcases Kapampangan creativity and craftsmanship and highlights an important Christmas tradition in the city.

Purposes:
  • To celebrate the Christmas season
  • To showcase Kapampangan creativity and craftsmanship
  • To promote local tourism and culture
